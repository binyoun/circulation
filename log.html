The engine before the hardware

Eleven weeks to VFCD. Before any cup exists, the interaction itself is the thing to get right, so this week starts with the logic, not the electronics.

  • The Five Elements (오행) mapping engine is written as a single specification: grip pressure, its trend, pulse rate, pulse regularity, and sustained hold read into one of the five elements and a colour. One spec, so the browser and the cup can never disagree.
  • A live simulator of three cups now runs that engine, with a synchronisation metric and a room colour that blooms as the three converge. Tuning the mapping needs no hardware. Open the system.
  • The cup firmware (ESP32, force sensor, pulse sensor, LED ring) is drafted to the same spec, ready for the bench.

The one experiment that decides the design: can the pulse sensor read a usable signal from a hand that is actively gripping? That is the first thing to test on the bench. If not, the reading leans on pressure and a brief "hold and be still" phase, which is coherent with the practice anyway.
